---
title: "Teams"
description: "Organize members into teams with shared resources and permissions."
---

Teams let you group organization members for access control, budget allocation, and usage analytics. They provide a layer of resource isolation within an organization.

<Note>
  Teams are available on the **Team** and **Enterprise** plans.
</Note>

## Creating a Team

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Teams">
    Go to **Teams** in the dashboard sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Team">
    Enter a name for your team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Members">
    Select organization members to add to the team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign Roles">
    Set each member as a lead or member within the team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Team Roles

Each team member has a role within the team:

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Lead** | Manages team settings, members, and budgets |
| **Member** | Uses team resources and views team analytics |

<Info>
  Team roles are separate from organization roles. A user can be a Developer at the organization level but a Lead within their team.
</Info>

## Team-Scoped Budgets

Assign budgets at the team level to control spending per group:

```
Organization: $1,000/month
  +-- Engineering Team: $500/month
  +-- Marketing Team: $300/month
  +-- Research Team: $200/month
```

Team budgets are enforced independently from organization and key-level budgets. When a team's budget is exhausted, all requests using that team's keys are blocked until the budget resets.

### Setting a Team Budget

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Budgets">
    Go to **Budgets** in the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a Budget">
    Select **Team** as the scope and choose the target team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Amount and Period">
    Configure the dollar amount and reset period (daily, weekly, or monthly).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Alert Threshold">
    Optionally set a percentage threshold for budget alerts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Team-Scoped Routing

Virtual keys can be assigned to a team. When a key is team-scoped:

- Usage is tracked under that team's analytics
- Team budgets apply to requests made with that key
- Team leads can manage the key

### Assigning Keys to Teams

When creating or editing a virtual key, select the team it belongs to:

```json
{
  "name": "engineering-prod",
  "environment": "live",
  "teamId": "team_abc123"
}
```

## Team Analytics

View usage analytics filtered by team to understand spending and usage patterns per group:

- **Request volume** -- How many requests each team makes
- **Token usage** -- Input and output tokens per team
- **Cost breakdown** -- Dollar spend per team per period
- **Model usage** -- Which models each team uses most

Navigate to **Analytics** and filter by team to see team-specific dashboards.

## Inviting Members to a Team

Organization members can be added to multiple teams. Only organization owners, admins, and team leads can manage team membership.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Team">
    Navigate to **Teams** and select the team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Member">
    Choose from existing organization members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Team Role">
    Assign the member as Lead or Member within the team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only users who are already members of the organization can be added to a team. Invite them to the organization first via the Team page.
</Warning>

## Removing Members

Team leads and organization admins can remove members from a team. Removing a member from a team does not remove them from the organization.
